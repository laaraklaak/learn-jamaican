Lesson 1: The Building Blocks

Welcome! If you can speak Jamaican, you already know the most important part. This lesson will connect your voice to the written word using the official Cassidy-JLU writing system.

The Jamaican Alphabet

The writing system uses the familiar English alphabet, but it is much more consistent. One letter always makes one sound. You'll notice that the letters C, Q, and X are not used for writing Jamaican words. Instead, we use other letters to make their sounds.

New Combination Letters

In addition to the standard alphabet, there are five special two-letter combinations that represent a single sound. Think of these as new letters in our alphabet.

CH
SH
ZH
NG
KW
  • CH makes a 'ch' sound (e.g., choch for 'church').
  • SH makes a 'sh' sound (e.g., shuu for 'shoe').
  • ZH makes a 'zh' sound, like the 's' in 'vision' (e.g., vizhan).
  • NG makes a 'ng' sound (e.g., sing).
  • KW makes a 'kw' sound (e.g., kwiin for 'queen').

The Vowel Sounds

Jamaican has five basic, pure vowel sounds, which are always short. Unlike in English, these vowels don't change their sound. Let's look at each one.

Vowel Sounds Like Example Word In a Sentence
a 'ah' as in 'father' gyal Di gyal a sing. (The girl is singing.)
e 'eh' as in 'bed' dem A fi dem buk. (It's their book.)
i 'ee' as in 'see' likl Gi mi a likl wata. (Give me a little water.)
o 'o' as in 'go' kot Im kot di trii. (He cut the tree.)
u 'oo' as in 'good' gud Dat de gud. (That is good.)

Long Vowels

To make a vowel sound longer, you simply write it twice. This is another way the writing system is very consistent.

  • aa makes a longer 'ah' sound. Example: baal (ball).
  • ee makes a longer 'eh' sound. Example: gweehn (going).
  • ii makes a longer 'ee' sound. Example: iit (eat).
  • oo makes a longer 'oh' sound. Example: eloo (hello).
  • uu makes a longer 'oo' sound. Example: shuuz (shoes).

Combination Vowels (Diphthongs)

Four vowel combinations are commonly used to make distinct sounds where two vowel sounds glide into one another.

  • IE makes an 'ay' sound, like in the English word 'gate'. Example: giet (gate).
  • UO makes an 'u-ho' sound, like in the English word 'boat'. Example: buot (boat).
  • AI makes an 'eye' sound, like in the English word 'buy'. Example: bait (bite).
  • OU makes an 'ow' sound, like in the English word 'cow'. Example: bout (about).
