Our Story, Our Language

This journey began with a simple but powerful idea: if you can speak Jamaican, you can learn to read and write it.

A Course for Us

This course is designed for you—the Jamaican speaker. Whether you are young or old, if you grew up speaking our beautiful language but never had the chance to see it written down, this is your space to learn. For too long, many of us were told that our language was "broken" or "improper." We were taught to be ashamed of our mother tongue, the very language that shaped our thoughts, our humour, and our heart.

"The ability to read and write your native language is a basic human right. It is the key to unlocking our own stories, our own literature, and our own power."

A Language of Strength and Structure

The truth is, the Jamaican language is not broken. It is a complete, expressive, and structured language, born from the resilience and creativity of our ancestors. Its journey from an oral tradition to a written form has been championed by cultural heroes and brilliant linguists.

Cultural icon, the Hon. Louise "Miss Lou" Bennett-Coverley, fought to give our language the respect it deserves, celebrating its richness on stage and screen. At the same time, linguists like Frederic Cassidy and the Jamaican Language Unit (JLU) at UWI worked tirelessly to formally document its structure, creating the consistent writing system you are learning today.

Why This Matters

Learning to read and write in your own language is an act of empowerment. It allows you to read the Bible for yourself, write a card to a loved one, send a text message with perfect clarity, and engage with the growing body of Jamaican literature. It connects you to your heritage and gives you the confidence to express yourself fully, in speech and in writing.

Welcome to the journey. It's time to own our language.
