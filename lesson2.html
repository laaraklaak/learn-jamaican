Lesson 2: The Rules of Writing

This lesson is your reference guide for the most important spelling rules. Mastering these is the key to writing with confidence.

Rules for Specific Letters

Some English letters are replaced entirely, while others have very specific rules.

  • The Letter 'c': This letter is never used by itself. For the "k" sound in "cat," use k (kyat). For the "s" sound in "cell," use s (sel).
  • The Letter 'h': This letter is never used alone. It only appears in the blends ch, sh, zh, and hn. The word "hat" becomes at.
  • The Letter 'g': The letter 'g' always has a hard sound, as in "goat". It never makes a soft "j" sound like in the English word "gem".
  • The Letter 'j': This letter is used for two main sounds: the "j" in "judge" (joj) and to replace the English 'dr' sound (jres for "dress").
  • The 'ng' Sound: This is the same as in "sing". However, if you hear a hard "g" sound *after* the 'ng', you must write it. Compare sing (no hard g) with fing-ga (finger).
  • The Letter 'k': Besides replacing the hard 'c', the letter 'k' also replaces the 'ck' blend. For example, "lick" is written lik.
  • The Letter 'q': We never use 'q'. This sound is always written as kw. For example, "queen" is kwiin.
  • The Letter 'x': This letter is replaced by the sounds it makes. Use ks for the sound in "box" (boks) and gz for the sound in "exam" (egzam).
  • 's' vs. 'z': This depends on the sound. 's' is a soft, hissing sound with no vibration in your throat. 'z' is a hard, buzzing sound with vibration. Compare ans (aunts) with anz (hands).

Key Spelling Principles

These broader rules help with word construction and endings.

  • The "OR" Rule: English sounds like 'er', 'ir', and 'ur' are almost always written as or (e.g., "work" is work, "girl" is gorl).
  • Consonant Glides: Use y or w between a consonant and a vowel to create a gliding sound (e.g., "car" is kyar, "boy" is bwai).
  • Word Endings: Words do not end with 'y' or 'w'. For an 'ee' sound at the end, use i ("money" is moni). For an 'eye' sound, use ai ("try" is chrai).
